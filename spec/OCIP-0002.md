## Abstract

OCIP-0002 defines the **Peer Governance and Capability Handshake Layer** — a protocol for node discovery, capability declaration, peer-to-peer capability verification, and collaboration agreement formalization within the OCIP and Inferix network.

OCIP-0002 enables:

- **Discovery** — nodes announce themselves on the network with structured capability declarations
- **Connection** — two nodes establish a network-level connection
- **Challenge** — optional, quota-controlled, mutual capability verification driven by each party's own expectations
- **Agreement** — a formalized, expiring collaboration record created only when both parties independently accept
- **Revocation** — either party may revoke an active agreement at any time via a signed notice

OCIP-0002 is the governance layer that sits between Inferix (compute) and OCIP-0003 (group sessions). It is not required for anonymous compute contribution. It is required when two parties wish to establish a verified collaborative relationship.

## 1. Motivation

The OCIP and Inferix network supports two classes of participation:

**Anonymous contribution** — a node contributes idle compute to the network, earns GFT credits, and draws on network inference. No identity, no declaration, no agreement required. This is the baseline and is fully supported without OCIP-0002.

**Verified collaboration** — two parties wish to work together on shared AI-assisted problems using their specialized models and pooled compute. They need to know whether the other party's model can actually do what it claims. They need an agreed scope. They need a record of what was verified and accepted.

OCIP-0002 fills this second need. It defines how parties find each other, how they verify each other's capabilities on their own terms, and how they formalize a collaboration — all without a central authority.

The core insight driving the design: **the problem is not malice, it is epistemic humility.** A node may genuinely believe its model is capable of something it is not. The challenge protocol exists not to catch liars but to surface what neither party knows they don't know — and to let each party set their own standard for what is good enough.

## 2. Design Principles

**1. Discovery without gatekeeping.** Any node can be discovered. Discovery does not require a handshake. Anonymous compute nodes are discoverable as compute resources. The handshake is a layer on top, not a prerequisite.

**2. Challenge is optional and mutual.** Neither party can demand a challenge the other has not agreed to. Each party controls their own challenge quota — the maximum number of questions they will answer. No party can force the other to respond to more than they allow.

**3. Evaluation is sovereign and private.** Each party evaluates challenge responses against their own expectations. There is no shared scoring system, no pass/fail threshold, no third-party judge. Each party decides independently whether they are satisfied.

**4. Agreement requires mutual acceptance.** A collaboration agreement is only formalized when both parties independently signal acceptance. Rejection by either party ends the process cleanly. No negotiation, no appeals.

**5. Taxonomy is structured but extensible.** Domain declarations follow a two-level taxonomy defined in this spec. The taxonomy is open for community extension. Nodes MAY declare themselves `unclassified` — this is a valid first-class state, not a missing field.

**6. Expiry is set by the initiator.** The party that initiates the handshake sets the agreement expiry. The receiving party accepts or declines including the proposed expiry. Renewal requires a new handshake cycle.

**7. Institutions govern their own boundaries.** The protocol does not impose governance. Institutions that require challenge completion before permitting collaboration MAY enforce this at their own boundary. The spec provides the mechanism. Policy is the institution's concern.

**8. Revocation is immediate and unilateral.** Either party may revoke an active agreement at any time without reason. Revocation takes effect immediately upon the other party receiving a valid `RevocationNotice`.

## 4. Governance Modes

OCIP-0002 supports three governance modes. The mode is determined by the participating nodes' configuration, not by the protocol itself.

### Mode 1 — Anonymous

No handshake. No declaration. The node contributes compute and earns GFT. It may be queried by others in sessions without any prior agreement. This is the default for individual contributors who have not opted into the handshake layer.

OCIP-0002 handshake objects are not used in this mode. Nodes operating in anonymous mode MAY still publish a `DiscoveryRecord` with `accepts_connections: false` to make their compute resources visible.

### Mode 2 — Voluntary Handshake

Two parties mutually choose to run the handshake before collaborating. Both may or may not include a challenge phase. Either party may skip the challenge by setting their quota to zero. The agreement is formalized only if both accept.

This is the default mode when two parties initiate a connection via the discovery mechanism.

### Mode 3 — Governed Handshake

An institution requires that any node connecting under its umbrella complete a capability handshake — including a challenge phase — before being permitted to participate in governed sessions. The institution enforces this policy at its boundary. The protocol provides the mechanism. Non-compliant nodes are not admitted to governed sessions but remain fully functional on the broader network.

Governed sessions MUST reference a valid, non-expired `CollaborationAgreement` `agreement_id` in the OCIP-0003 `session.metadata`.

## 5. Protocol Overview

```
Phase 0 — Discovery
  Node A publishes DiscoveryRecord to network
  Node B queries discovery index via DiscoveryQuery
  Node B receives DiscoveryQueryResult containing Node A's record

Phase 1 — Connection  [timeout: 48 hours]
  A → B: ConnectionRequest
  B → A: ConnectionResponse (accept | decline)
  [if declined: process ends]

Phase 2 — Challenge (optional, mutual, quota-controlled)  [timeout: 72 hours]
  A → B: ChallengeEnvelope (up to B's declared quota)
  B → A: ChallengeEnvelope (up to A's declared quota)
  B runs A's challenges against B's model → ChallengeResponse to A
  A runs B's challenges against A's model → ChallengeResponse to B

Phase 3 — Evaluation (private, sovereign)  [timeout: 72 hours]
  A evaluates B's ChallengeResponse → ChallengeResult (internal, not transmitted)
  B evaluates A's ChallengeResponse → ChallengeResult (internal, not transmitted)
  A signals: accept | reject
  B signals: accept | reject
  [if either rejects: process ends, no agreement]

Phase 4 — Agreement
  [if both accept]
  Initiator produces CollaborationAgreement
  Both parties countersign
  Agreement stored locally by both parties
  agreement_id available for reference in OCIP-0003 session.metadata

Revocation (any time after Phase 4)
  Either party → Other party: RevocationNotice
  Agreement treated as expired immediately upon receipt
```

## 6. Capability Taxonomy

OCIP-0002 uses a two-level domain taxonomy for capability declarations. The taxonomy is open for community extension via the OCIP repository. Implementations MUST accept unknown domain or subdomain values and treat them as valid declarations rather than rejecting them.

A node that does not wish to declare any specific capability MUST use `domain: "unclassified"`. This is a valid first-class state. Unclassified nodes are fully discoverable and fully functional.

### Top-Level Domains and Sub-Domains

| Domain | Sub-Domains |
|---|---|
| `medicine` | `pharmacology`, `clinical-trials`, `medical-imaging`, `genomics`, `public-health`, `regulatory-compliance` |
| `law` | `regulatory-compliance`, `contract-analysis`, `intellectual-property`, `litigation`, `international-law` |
| `engineering` | `software`, `hardware`, `civil`, `mechanical`, `electrical`, `systems-design` |
| `science` | `physics`, `chemistry`, `biology`, `materials`, `environmental`, `data-science` |
| `education` | `curriculum-design`, `assessment`, `pedagogy`, `research-methodology` |
| `finance` | `risk-analysis`, `accounting`, `investment`, `regulatory-compliance`, `insurance` |
| `technology` | `distributed-systems`, `machine-learning`, `cybersecurity`, `networking`, `cloud-infrastructure` |
| `humanities` | `history`, `linguistics`, `philosophy`, `literature`, `ethics` |
| `policy` | `public-policy`, `international-relations`, `urban-planning`, `environmental-policy` |
| `unclassified` | *(no sub-domains — used when no specific domain is declared)* |
| `other` | *(free-form — use `specialization_notes` to describe)* |

Sub-domains are RECOMMENDED but not REQUIRED. A node MAY declare a top-level domain without specifying sub-domains. This taxonomy is at version 1.0 and is expected to grow through community contributions. Implementations MUST NOT reject declarations containing unknown domain or subdomain values.

### 7.14 RevocationNotice

A signed notice terminating an active `CollaborationAgreement` before its natural expiry. Either party MAY issue a revocation at any time without providing a reason. Revocation takes effect immediately upon the other party receiving a valid notice.

| Field | Type | Required | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| `revocation_id` | string (UUID) | **REQUIRED** | Unique identifier for this revocation notice. |
| `ocip_spec` | string | **REQUIRED** | MUST be `"OCIP-0002"`. |
| `agreement_id` | string (UUID) | **REQUIRED** | The `agreement_id` of the `CollaborationAgreement` being revoked. |
| `revoking_node_id` | string (UUID) | **REQUIRED** | The `node_id` of the party issuing this revocation. MUST be either the `initiator.node_id` or `receiver.node_id` of the referenced agreement. |
| `created_at` | string (ISO 8601) | **REQUIRED** | When this revocation was issued. |
| `reason` | string | OPTIONAL | A human-readable reason for revocation. Not required. |
| `signature` | string | OPTIONAL | Cryptographic signature of this notice using the revoking node's `public_key`. RECOMMENDED. Implementations SHOULD verify signatures on received revocation notices. |

Implementations MUST propagate received revocation notices promptly. An agreement that has been revoked MUST be treated as expired immediately — it MUST NOT be used to authorize new governed sessions.

## 8. Discovery Mechanism

### 8.1 Node Announcement

A node announces itself to the network by publishing a `DiscoveryRecord` to the discovery layer. The discovery layer is a queryable index maintained by the Inferix coordination layer (see §8.3).

Nodes SHOULD re-publish their `DiscoveryRecord` when:

- Their `SpecializationDeclaration` changes (model update, domain change, quota change)
- Their `accepts_connections` status changes
- Their `governance_mode` changes
- The record has not been refreshed within 30 days

Nodes MUST NOT publish `DiscoveryRecords` on behalf of other nodes.

### 8.2 Discovery Query

A node searching for collaborators submits a `DiscoveryQuery` to the discovery layer and receives a `DiscoveryQueryResult`. The query and result objects are defined in §7.5 and §7.6.

**Discovery does not imply any obligation.** A node appearing in discovery results is not required to accept connection requests. The `accepts_connections` field on a `DiscoveryRecord` is the authoritative signal of current availability.

**Rate limiting:** Implementations SHOULD rate-limit discovery queries per querying node to prevent abuse of the index.

### 8.3 Inferix Integration

The Inferix coordination layer implements the OCIP-0002 discovery mechanism as follows:

- The coordination layer maintains a **discovery index** — a queryable store of active `DiscoveryRecord` objects
- Contributor nodes that opt into OCIP-0002 publish their `DiscoveryRecord` to the coordination layer at registration and on update
- The coordination layer exposes a query endpoint accepting `DiscoveryQuery` objects and returning `DiscoveryQueryResult` objects
- Anonymous contributor nodes (governance mode `"anonymous"`) MAY appear in discovery results but MUST have `accepts_connections: false`
- The coordination layer does not validate the content of `SpecializationDeclaration` fields — it indexes and returns them as published

Other discovery implementations (e.g. DHT-based, federated directory) are permitted provided they conform to the `DiscoveryRecord`, `DiscoveryQuery`, and `DiscoveryQueryResult` schemas defined in this spec.

## 9. Handshake Protocol

### 9.1 Phase 1 — Connection

1. Node A (initiator) queries the discovery layer and identifies Node B as a potential collaborator.
2. A sends a `ConnectionRequest` to B, including `proposed_expiry` and `challenge_requested`.
3. B receives the request and decides to accept or decline.
   - If B declines: B sends `ConnectionResponse` with `decision: "decline"`. Process ends.
   - If B accepts: B sends `ConnectionResponse` with `decision: "accept"` and `challenge_accepted` set.
4. If both A set `challenge_requested: true` and B set `challenge_accepted: true`, proceed to Phase 2.
5. If either party opts out of challenges, proceed directly to Phase 3.

**Timeout:** See §9.5.

### 9.2 Phase 2 — Challenge (Optional)

Challenge is mutual. Both parties send challenges to each other independently and simultaneously.

1. A prepares a `ChallengeEnvelope` with questions for B. Items MUST NOT exceed B's `challenge_quota`.
2. B prepares a `ChallengeEnvelope` with questions for A. Items MUST NOT exceed A's `challenge_quota`.
3. A sends its envelope to B. B sends its envelope to A.
4. B runs A's prompts against B's model and sends a `ChallengeResponse` to A.
5. A runs B's prompts against A's model and sends a `ChallengeResponse` to B.

**Quota enforcement:** A node receiving a `ChallengeEnvelope` with more items than its declared `challenge_quota` MUST respond with an error and is not required to answer excess items. The sender MUST reduce the challenge before resubmitting.

**No partial responses:** A `ChallengeResponse` MUST contain one entry per `ChallengeItem`. Unanswerable items MUST use `"response": "no_response"`.

**Timeout:** See §9.5.

### 9.3 Phase 3 — Evaluation

Evaluation is private. `ChallengeResult` objects are NEVER transmitted.

1. A evaluates B's `ChallengeResponse` and records a local `ChallengeResult`.
2. B evaluates A's `ChallengeResponse` and records a local `ChallengeResult`.
3. A signals its decision: `accept` or `reject`.
4. B signals its decision: `accept` or `reject`.
5. If either signals `reject`: process ends. No agreement is created.
6. If both signal `accept`: proceed to Phase 4.

Evaluation is final. There is no re-challenge, no negotiation, and no appeal. A rejected handshake may be retried from Phase 1 at any time by mutual consent.

**Timeout:** See §9.5.

### 9.4 Phase 4 — Agreement

1. The initiator (A) produces a `CollaborationAgreement` with `expires_at` matching `proposed_expiry`.
2. A signs and sends it to B.
3. B countersigns and returns it to A.
4. Both parties store the agreement locally.
5. The `agreement_id` is now available for use in OCIP-0003 `session.metadata` and OCIP-0004 `project.metadata`.

### 9.5 Phase Timeouts

Implementations MUST handle timeouts gracefully. If a phase times out, the handshake MUST be treated as failed and the initiator SHOULD be notified.

| Phase | Recommended timeout | Behavior on timeout |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 — awaiting `ConnectionResponse` | 48 hours | Initiator treats request as declined. No retry is automatic. |
| Phase 2 — awaiting `ChallengeResponse` | 72 hours | Awaiting party treats challenge as unanswered. MAY proceed to Phase 3 with unanswered challenge treated as a failed evaluation, or MAY abort the handshake. |
| Phase 3 — awaiting acceptance signal | 72 hours | Awaiting party treats the other party as having rejected. Process ends. |

These are RECOMMENDED values. Implementations MAY use shorter timeouts and SHOULD document the timeouts they apply.

## 10. File Format

- All OCIP-0002 objects MUST be encoded as UTF-8.
- All OCIP-0002 objects MUST be valid JSON per RFC 8259.
- Recommended file extensions:
  - Discovery record: `.ocip4-discovery.json`
  - Collaboration agreement: `.ocip4-agreement.json`
  - Revocation notice: `.ocip4-revocation.json`
- Recommended MIME type: `application/ocip+json`
- The `ocip_spec` field MUST be `"OCIP-0002"` where present.

## 11. Conformance

### Discovery Conformance

A conforming discovery implementation MUST:

- Accept and index valid `DiscoveryRecord` objects
- Accept `DiscoveryQuery` objects and return `DiscoveryQueryResult` objects
- Support filtering by `domain`, `node_type`, and `accepts_connections`
- NOT validate the content of `SpecializationDeclaration` fields
- Accept unknown domain and subdomain values without rejecting records

### Handshake Conformance

A conforming handshake implementation MUST:

- Implement all four handshake phases
- Enforce challenge quota on incoming `ChallengeEnvelope` objects
- Require one `ChallengeResponse` entry per `ChallengeItem`
- NOT transmit `ChallengeResult` objects to the other party
- NOT create a `CollaborationAgreement` unless both parties signal acceptance
- Store `CollaborationAgreement` locally upon completion
- Implement phase timeouts as defined in §9.5
- Accept and process `RevocationNotice` objects
- Treat agreements referenced by a valid `RevocationNotice` as immediately expired

A conforming handshake implementation SHOULD:

- Support cryptographic signing of all transmitted objects
- Verify signatures on received objects when `public_key` is declared
- Surface `evaluation_criteria` from received challenges to the user before response generation

### Institutional Governance Conformance

An institution implementing governed sessions MUST:

- Require a valid, non-expired `CollaborationAgreement` before admitting nodes to governed OCIP-0003 sessions
- Reject nodes whose declaration version has changed since the agreement was signed without renewal
- Surface agreement expiry warnings to participants before `expires_at`
- Propagate received `RevocationNotice` objects to all affected session participants promptly

## 12. Privacy and Security Considerations

### Identity is User-Controlled

`display_name` and `node_id` are self-declared. OCIP-0002 does not provide identity verification. Cryptographic signing via `public_key` provides integrity verification but not identity verification. Implementations requiring verified identity MUST use an external authentication layer.

### Challenge Content

Challenge prompts are authored by the evaluating party and sent to the challenged party's model. Challenge content MUST NOT include credentials, private keys, or system-level secrets. Implementations SHOULD warn users before sending challenges containing potentially sensitive content.

### Evaluation Privacy

`ChallengeResult` objects are private records. Implementations MUST NOT transmit evaluation results to the other party. The acceptance or rejection signal conveys only the binary outcome.

### Declaration Honesty

OCIP-0002 cannot enforce honest declarations. The challenge protocol provides surface-level verification but cannot guarantee a node's model performs as declared under all conditions. Collaborating parties accept residual uncertainty and SHOULD scope agreements narrowly to verified capabilities.

### Agreement Integrity

`CollaborationAgreement` objects SHOULD be cryptographically signed by both parties. Implementations SHOULD verify signatures before admitting nodes to governed sessions. An unsigned agreement is valid but provides no tamper protection.

### Revocation Security

`RevocationNotice` objects SHOULD be cryptographically signed. Implementations that cannot verify a revocation signature MUST surface a warning rather than silently accepting or rejecting the notice. Implementations MUST NOT allow a third party to revoke an agreement on behalf of either party without a valid signature from one of the agreement parties.

### No Credentials

Implementations MUST NOT store API keys, tokens, passwords, wallet credentials, or private keys in any OCIP-0002 field.

---

## 13. Versioning

OCIP-0002 follows the same versioning policy as OCIP-0005. See OCIP-0005 §10.

When a node's `SpecializationDeclaration` is updated, the `declaration_id` changes and `version` MUST be incremented. Existing `CollaborationAgreement` objects referencing the prior `declaration_id` remain valid until `expires_at` but SHOULD be flagged for renewal by both parties.

## 14. Relationship to Other Specs

| Spec | Relationship |
|---|---|
| **OCIP-0005** | OCIP-0002 is independent of the conversation layer. A collaboration agreement does not require or produce an OCIP-0005 document. |
| **OCIP-0004** | A project (OCIP-0004) MAY reference a `CollaborationAgreement` by `agreement_id` in `project.metadata` to record the governance basis for the project. |
| **OCIP-0003** | Group sessions MAY reference a `CollaborationAgreement` by `agreement_id` in `session.metadata`. Governed sessions MUST include a valid, non-expired `agreement_id`. The `InferenceCost` settlement in OCIP-0003 is independent of agreement status. OCIP-0002 `NodeIdentity.node_id` values MAY be linked to OCIP-0003 `participant_id` values via `participant.metadata.ocip4_node_id`. |
| **Inferix** | The Inferix coordination layer implements the OCIP-0002 discovery index. The `inferix_node_id` in `NodeIdentity` links a governance identity to a compute identity. Anonymous Inferix contributors are not required to publish a `DiscoveryRecord`. |
